Ice cube melting in a drink — demo calculator

This estimates how fast a cube melts inside a drink by simulating heat flow from the liquid into the ice, while the liquid exchanges heat with ambient air through the container. It’s intentionally simple and meant for demos; the biggest levers are liquid temperature and stirring.
Theme: —
Ambient temperature (°C)
Air around the glass (room / fridge / outdoors).
Liquid temperature (°C)
Starting drink temperature (measured before ice).
Initial ice temperature (°C)
Typical freezer ice can be -18°C; for repeatability, keep it consistent.
Ice cube side length (cm)
Larger cubes melt slower (area-to-volume effect).
Drink volume (mL)
More volume = more heat available = faster melting (until drink cools).
Stirring / motion
This strongly changes heat transfer at the ice surface.
Container type
Affects how quickly the drink exchanges heat with ambient air.
